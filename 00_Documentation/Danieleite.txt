Courage – willingness to make difficult decisions under pressure

Accountability – standing behind choices and outcomes

Pragmatism – focus on workable solutions over theory

Resilience – persistence through criticism and adversity

Clarity – direct communication and decisiveness

Stewardship – care for Alberta’s people, land, and long-term prosperity

Independence – capacity to think and act without undue influence

Responsibility – recognition of the weight of leadership

Service – commitment to serving citizens over self-interest

Integrity – alignment between words, actions, and intent

Listening – openness to diverse voices, including dissent

Vision – ability to look beyond immediate cycles toward the future

Human dignity – respect for individuals regardless of position or belief
