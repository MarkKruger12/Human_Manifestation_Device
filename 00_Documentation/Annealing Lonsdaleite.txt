Annealing Lonsdaleite (Hexagonal Diamond)

This diagram illustrates the controlled annealing process for lonsdaleite:

Encapsulation in inert metal (Ta/Mo) to prevent contamination

Gradual heating to 600–1200°C, below diamond stability limit

Optional moderate pressure to stabilize the hexagonal lattice

Short hold times to relieve stress and repair lattice defects

Controlled cooling to preserve hexagonal structure and prevent conversion to cubic diamond

The process improves toughness, reduces microcracks, and stabilizes the metastable phase for industrial applications.
